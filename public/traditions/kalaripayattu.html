Kalaripayattu (कलारीपयट्टु)

1. Origin

Kalaripayattu is an ancient martial art that originated in Kerala, India, over 3,000 years ago. It is one of the oldest surviving fighting systems in existence. "Kalari" refers to the battlefield, and "Payattu" means fight or practice. This art form is said to have been taught by the warrior-sage Parasurama, an avatar of Lord Vishnu.

2. Techniques and Practices

Kalaripayattu encompasses a wide range of combat techniques, including strikes, kicks, grappling, and the use of various weapons like swords, spears, and daggers. The practice also includes an understanding of pressure points (Marmas), which can be used for healing or inflicting damage.

3. Connection to Yog and Ayurveda

Like Yog, Kalaripayattu integrates physical, mental, and spiritual training. Traditionally, Kalaripayattu warriors also learned Ayurvedic medicine to treat injuries and enhance their physical abilities. The martial art emphasizes flexibility, speed, agility, and awareness, with students beginning their training at a young age.

4. Modern Significance

Kalaripayattu is still practiced today in Kerala and has gained global recognition. It has influenced various forms of Indian dance, theater, and even modern martial arts. Although not as widespread as other martial arts, Kalaripayattu holds an important place in India's cultural heritage.
